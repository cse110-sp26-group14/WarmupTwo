### Raw Research
For this warm-up, we split the work such that every member of Team 14 would contribute to the research portion by doing their own personal research both into how slot machines work, what asepcts are commonly found in them, and the different actions taken to appeal to different demographics.  Additionally, each member was to do research on the different types of users who play these kinds of games as well as why they would be attracted to this specific hobby. 
### Research Overview
Overall, the main findings Team 14 came across after compiling the raw research altogether is that: 
- Players: Slot machine players often vary, ranging from gamblers who play for winning money to casual players who play for relaxation or social bonding with other players. For the purpose of this Warm-Up we chose to focus on casual players who play for fun and relaxation. 
- Domain/Functionality: Depending on the User, different slot machines incorporate or omit features to appeal to their desired audience, for example users who prefer casual games may enjoy a progression bar or specific theming being added to the slot machine compared to a gambler who may prefer simple functionality that quickly and clearly communicates wins and losses and consistent replayability. 
- Visuals: SLot machines typically adhere to a visual style that can appeal to general consistent users through a variety of methods. One such example is with coloring, where bright but distinct colors are often used to entice users to garner desired reactions. Furthermore, many slot machines utilize distinct symbols, cultural elements, themes and animations to make the slot machine enticing and engaging for a given user. 
- Audio: Auditory details are often split between two sections, the sound effects(sfx) and the music. When it comes to the sfx, many slot machines use loud clear effects to effectively communicate wins and losses and elicit the appropriate reaction out of a player through these sounds (exemplified with progression based "power-ups" being backed with satisfying sfx like "cha-ching"). With the game's music, often the machine will use energizing but relaxing music to subconciously calm users and keep them engaged to continuing to play the game, no matter the outcome of their previous spins. 
- Code Specifications: The slot machine's code created by the AI model should do it's best to consistently be considered clean code. To be considered clean code, the AI should do it's best to make it's output have a clear and concise architecture/structure, include comments desctibing the purpose, paramaters and return values of all functions, handle errors in such a way that the final product avoids crashing, include Test Cases that test for the general and edge cases, properly split different features of the product between working files and maintain a secure privacy to the user experience.

The persona documents and user stories have the explicit purpose of making our team consider the users who will be playing the generated product and think of what features and specific they'd want to see when playing our slot machine. 
- Persona Document: From all the persona documents created, we find that many of these personas all vary from age, demographic and motivation to play a slot machine. Despite this variation we were able to find that many of these personas fit under general groups of players who play for the thrill or players who play to attain more money, with variations as to why they follow under those categories.
- User Stories: Many user stories vary between all members of Team 14, but generally stay under the trend of describing the kind of player that is making this story, explaining what function they want out of their ideal slot machine, and explaining why they want this specific feature.
