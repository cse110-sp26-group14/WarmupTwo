Name: Johnathan Shade 

Details:   
Johnathan is a 64 year old retiree. After a messy divorce he finds that he had won the majority of the possessions he and his ex-wife had fought over and is now feeling overly confident in his luck, as to be frank he should *not* have won that dispute case. 

Wants and Needs:  
Given this newly found arrogance he decides he wants to take up gambling in the hopes of gaining even more. Even if the odds are low he truly believes he holds all the cards in his favor. All Johnathan Shade wants from his ideal slot machine is a way to go big and go home. If he can bet as much money as he reasonably can he’ll likely take it. 

Doesn’t Want/Doesn’t Need:   
Johnathan has noticed a lot of slot machines seem to have some payout that is rewarded the more he plays and progresses on a single slot machine. In his honest opinion he could care less about that big loadout, in fact it feels a bit cheap and undeserving if he just has to reach some specific point to get a big win. While he doesn’t mind it as much either he also feels he doesn’t need to be subjected to these grand themes that clutter up his screen seen in some machines. All he wants is to win and for it to be completely from the luck of the draw. 

User Story \#1   
As a chronic gambler with a lot of money in their hand, I want to be able to be able to place my own bets and have those bets give me massive returns so that when I win the slot machine gives me more money than what I started out with. 

User Story \#2  
As an older gentle-person who’s just looking to play, I want my plays be fast, flashy and clearly communicated to me so that I know for with absolute certainty when I win, when I lost and how close I was to winning to track my chances and see if I should keep going big or change up how I play in the game.
